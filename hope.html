Hope

Hope is the hush before the dawn,
A whisper when the night feels long,
The thread of gold in tattered seams,
The quiet pause beneath our dreams.

It's not the thunder, not the fire,
But steady hands that never tire.
It walks with us through shadowed land,
A flicker held in trembling hand.

Hope is the breath when words have fled,
The tears we've shed, yet lift our head.
It's planted deep in barren ground
And somehow still it makes a sound.

It doesn't boast or beg to stay,
But close like cones that won't decay,
A promise wrapped in fragile skin
That something good can still begin.

Hope is the light we dare to find,
When storms are loud and fate unkind,
It does not need the skies to clear—
It only asks that we draw near.
Julian Valdez
