Dear John

If a poet is in love with you,
You're sure to live forever.
Death may have a hold of you—
You may be gone physically,
But your traits, your essence
Will remain for eternity
Etched on the pages of a book,
Marked on people's minds.
Many students will recite you,
Some may even adore you
Like I do now.
Isn't it just wonderful?
That's why I strive to be a poet,
So I can make you immortal
Like Poe's beautiful Annabel Lee
And Elizabeth Browning's precious Robert,
My dear John, life will never leave you
As Coleridge's sweet Genevieve
And Keats' lovely Emma.
Julian Valdez
10.18.2018
