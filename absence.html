In Your Absence

I search the wind for whispers
Of the voice I knew so well
But silence fills the spaces
Where your laughter used to dwell.

The light that once so brightly shone
In the space we call our home
Now softly fades away
And leaves an emptiness unknown.

And all the warmth you left behind
Still lingers, though it sways
Like echoes in the evening wind,
Faint memories of brighter days.

Your hands that held me steady
Through the storms I couldn't bear
Are gone, and yet I feel them
In the memory of your care.

The world moves on without you
Though mine has come undone,
For in my heart's deep chambers
You remain my only sun.

I lose you in the moments
When I forget to call your name
Yet find you in the quiet
Where you live without a claim.

In grief, I walk the distance
That your love has left behind
For losing you, dear Nanay,
Is losing half my mind.
Julian Valdez
10.01.2024
In loving memory of the most selfless soul I have ever known—my mother, Nanay Delia Caballa y Amosco. Your love was quiet, tireless, and unconditional. You gave so much of yourself so that we could have more. I will carry your love with me always. Rest now in eternal peace.
