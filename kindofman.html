If I Were That Kind of Man

Answer to If You Let Me
I've built my walls with quiet stone—
The kind you just don't outgrow.
I've walked these paths so long alone
It's the only map I know.
They say there's comfort in the mess
But I've survived on something less,
And needing someone, truth be told,
Always felt too soft, too bold.
But you sit there like you're not afraid,
Like light can touch what time has frayed.

If I were that kind of man,
The kind who lets go of a plan,
I might reach out just for a while,
Let you in past the practiced smile.
I'd say your laugh feels like a breeze
In rooms I never learned to breathe.
But I'm not him, I never am.
Still, I notice when you stand.

I've seen the way your silence waits
Like patience might reward your faith.
And though I keep the world outside,
You found the crack I couldn't hide.
I tell myself it's nothing much—
A fluke, a flicker, just a touch.
But when you speak, the quiet bends
And I forget how silence ends.
You ask for nothing loud or fast,
Just presence, like it's built to last.

If I were that kind of man,
The kind who could reach out a hand,
I'd let you stay when nights get cold
And offer stories never told.
I'd say your light, it doesn't blind
But it lingers in the darker mind.
But I don't chase, not as I planned.
Still, I listen when you stand.

Maybe there's a version of me
That doesn't flinch when he feels seen,
Maybe in some quieter place,
He lets her touch the unlit space.

If I were that kind of man,
Who still believes that someone can
Sit with the echoes of the truth,
I'd let that someone be you.
But I've grown used to staying still,
Too far gone, or maybe just too willful.
Yet when you're near, I understand,
The cold is less where you stand.
I don't say much, I never do—
But I see you, that much is true.
Julian Valdez
11.03.2025
