Only One Home

I've walked the streets where towers gleam,
And sailed through cities made of dreams.
I've tasted life in finer ways,
And watched the sun in distant bays.
But none of it can still the sound
Of waves that pull me gently down—
To shores I knew when I was small
Where the sea and sky would softly call.

For there's only one home I know
Where the ricefields stretch and the cool winds blow,
Where the hills rise green and the birds still sing,
And joy is found in the simplest things.
I've roamed so far, but still I see
My heart was left beside that sea.
The world is wide, but I have known...
There is only one home.

I've danced beneath a thousand lights,
Shared laughter deep into the nights,
Met strangers turn to friends for years,
And chased down dreams through hope and fears.
But somewhere in a quiet breeze,
I hear the hush of mountain trees.
And in the scent of ocean spray,
My childhood calls me home someday.

For there's only one home I know
Where my mother's smile was soft and slow.
Where my father's voice was warm and wide,
Like stories told by candlelight.
The roads are long, the world is fast
But I still long for what won't pass.
No place I go, no life I own...
Will ever be my home.

Yes, there's only one home I see—
Where my soul runs wild and my heart runs free,
Where the sea still sings and the mountains stand,
And love grows quiet, close at hand.
The world can shine, the stars can roam
But they can't change the pull of home.
I've travelled far, but now I've grown
To know there's only one home.
Julian Valdez
