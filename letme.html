If You Let Me

Answered by If I Were That Kind of Man
You move through life like it's a fight,
With worn-out words and guarded light.
You push away what others crave
As if needing love would make you cave.
You sneer at love, dismiss romance
Like kindness is a foolish dance,
But I've seen your silence speak so loud
The way you care without the crowd.
You hold the world at arms' length still—
I would stand closer if you will.

If you let me, I'd be quiet
Like a light that doesn't riot,
I'd sit beside your storm and wait,
No need for grand or perfect fate.
I'm just a girl you barely see,
But I'd love you, gently, if you let me.

You wear your solitude like pride,
A shield you never set aside,
But once I saw your warmth show
When you fed that stray so quiet, slow.
You smiled, but then you turned away
As if your softness might betray
The man they think you have to be—
But that's the man who wrecked in me.
I'm not the flame that lights your night,
But I'd be there when shadows bite.

If you let me, I'd be near,
Not to fix—just to be there.
I don't need some fairy tale,
Just the part of you you never tell.
I'm just a girl you'll never need,
But I'd love you, gently, if you let me.

I know you'd never ask for a hand to hold—
You stood too long out in the cold,
But if one day the silence gets too heavy,
I'd be the one still holding steady.
If you let me.

If you let me, I'd be kind,
Not to fix—just stay behind,
No promises, no plans to sway,
Just loving you the honest way.
No hopes to change what makes you true,
I love you simply because you're you.
And I'd love you truly,
If you let me.
Julian Valdez
